## The Problem

Hospitality Purchasing Solutions is a restaurant consulting firm that helps independent operators reduce purchasing costs through group buying power. The business had no web presence — all leads came through word of mouth and direct outreach. They needed a site that clearly communicated their services and converted visitors into consultation requests.

## My Approach

I started by interviewing the business owner to understand the sales conversation: what questions prospects ask, what objections come up, and what information closes deals. The site architecture mirrors this sales flow.

**Content-first design:** Rather than starting with visual design, I structured the content around the most common prospect questions: What do you do? How does it work? What does it cost? The FAQ section directly addresses objections (hidden fees, contract requirements, switching costs).

**SEO foundation:** Implemented JSON-LD schema markup for local business and service listings to establish search presence from day one. Structured data gives the site a head start on competing with established competitors.

**Clear service hierarchy:** The four core services (group purchasing, menu engineering, menu pricing, operations consulting) each get dedicated sections with specific outcomes and value propositions.

## The Outcome

The site launched with a professional, content-focused design that accurately represents the business. It provides a credible digital presence for a company that previously relied entirely on offline relationships, and gives prospects a clear path to engage.

## What I Learned

Small business websites are a good exercise in constraint. No budget for custom photography, no time for elaborate animations, no room for ambiguity. Every element has to earn its place by moving a prospect closer to picking up the phone.
